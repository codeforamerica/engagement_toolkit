About This Project

My block is a mess and really just needs a small group of people to get together and pick it up in an afternoon. I know some cities help out with projects like this, and I want to organize a few of my friends to pick up this rubbish.

How to get my street cleaned up in San Francisco

by FAQ Robot

  1. Call 311 to see if people are already involved with cleanups on your street via the Adopt-A-Street program.

  2. Recall that the law requires property owners/ground floor tenants to keep their adjacent sidewalk clean of trash and leaves. Contact 311 if there is a violation.

  3. Attempt to join San Francisco’s Adopt-A-Street Program.

    Organize merchants and neighbors on your street. They all have a strong incentive to see the street cleaned.

    If you are an individual, you can make an agreement with San Francisco to clean up your street. Merchant and neighborhood associations can also make an agreement with San Francisco to clean up a street.

    (If you are having trouble finding volunteers or other partners, contact an organization like A Good Idea - http://agoodideasf.org. They may be able to connect you with willing volunteers.)

    The Department of Public Works will provide supplies for you (e.g., brooms, rakes, bags, gloves).

    To sign up for the Adopt-A-Street program, email volunteer@sfdpw.org or fill out this form: http://38.106.4.205/ftp/uploadedfiles/sfdpw/director/Adopt_a_Street_(application_only).pdf

    Send your application form to: 2323 Cesar Chavez Street San Francisco, CA 94124-1091
